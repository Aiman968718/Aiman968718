# Week 1 — Reflection

## Topic Covered: Computer Science in Society

## What I Learned

This week gave me an introduction to the wider role of Computer Science 
in shaping today's society, covering areas like healthcare, education, 
communication, and business. The main thing I took away was that 
technology is not just about writing code, it is really about solving 
everyday problems and making people's lives better. I also learned about 
the responsibility that developers have when building systems that are 
ethical, inclusive, and focused on the needs of the user.

## Key Insight

Every technology we build has a social side to it. As developers, we need 
to think beyond just making something that works and also consider the 
wider effect it has on users, communities, and ethical values throughout 
the entire software development process.

## Real-Life Application

This way of thinking directly affects how I will approach my own software 
projects. Building applications that respect data privacy, accessibility, 
and ethical standards is not just a choice, it is a professional 
responsibility. From now on, I plan to keep these principles in mind at 
every stage of development, from the initial planning all the way to 
deployment.
